<div class="textcontainer"> <p class="margin"> </p> <h3>week 3: hand tools and fabrication</h3> <h4>final kinetic sculpture (blooming flower)</h4> <h4>process</h4> i began by sketching out some ideas for the flower shape and how it should move. i took inspiration from this video of a swing. based off this, it seemed like a similar mechanism could allow the flower petal to move back and forth. i went through a couple iterations, shown below. <br></br> then, i designed the component in fusion 360 to be laser cut. i wanted a simple shape to latch onto the spinning part of the motor, so i measured the dimensions of the shape and put that in fusion 360. since i was unsure how the cardboard kerf would affect the final dimensions and i needed the cutout to fit very tightly, i laser cut a couple prototypes to check the dimensions. i also started out with TODO mm cardboard. i realized that the laser would burn through part of the cardboard hole and the cardboard was too flimsy, so i opted for 6mm cardboard instead. <br></br> once i had some prototypes, i played around with how the petal should move. i decided to use a thin wire to connect the petal to the spinning part of the motor. i then added a cutout in the base of the petal's stem so that the wire could fit through. , but eventually opted for 6mm for more structure. <br></br> </div>
