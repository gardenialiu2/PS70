final project: interactive flower dress

project overview

i've designed a dress that incorporates 3D printed elements and servo-controlled petals. the dress consists of a 3D printed top and a skirt with interactive flower petals. the flower petals will be controlled by servo motors, creating movement and interactivity in the garment.

petal mechanism

i designed the petal mechanism in fusion 360. the petals need to be attached to the servo motors in a way that allows them to move naturally. i created a design with attachment points that can connect to the servo motor arm.

development plan

for MVP0, i'm aiming to get an initial iteration of the petals on the skirt as well as the 3d printed top done. i will also sew a small scaled-down version of the skirt to ensure my process can be used for the final skirt. i also included the initial fabrics that i'll be using for MVP0. once i've gotten the electronics to work properly, i will use a different material to make the dress more sturdy.

development schedule:
  • week 10/9-10/16: attach servo motors and petals, print 3d printed fabric for the top
  • week 10/16-10/23: have working petals and initial 3d printed top, finish MVP0
  • week 10/23-10/30: sew inner lining for the top
  • week 10/30-11/6: sew inner liner of the skirt
  • week 11/6-11/13: sew outside layers of the skirt
  • week 11/13-11/20: refine petal materials
  • week 11/20-11/27: integrate electronics into the dress
  • week 11/27-12/4: final assembly, testing and refinement
  • week 12/4-12/10: finished product!

bill of materials

  • ESP32 microcontroller
  • servo motors
  • TPU flexible filament for final top outer fabric
  • cotton and mesh fabric for skirt base
  • batteries
  • laser-cut acrylic/wood for petal mechanisms
  • cardboard for prototyping
